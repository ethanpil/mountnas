# Logs (RAM by default; optional persistence for post-mortems)
View:            nas logs            (last 100 lines)
Follow:          nas logs -f
Supervisor log:  cat /var/log/mountnas.log

Logs live in RAM and vanish at reboot — after a crash or power cut there is
nothing to read. Opt in to persistent logging on the data disk:

  nas logs --persist on      -> /mnt/nasdata/logs/messages (1 MB x 10 files)
  nas logs --persist off
  nas commit                 (persist the setting itself)

Tradeoff: periodic log writes keep the data disk awake — if disk spin-down
matters to you, leave persistence off and rely on 'nas report' while the
box is still up.
