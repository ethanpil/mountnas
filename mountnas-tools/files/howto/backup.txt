# Backups: the boot USB, your config, and your data
THREE different things — know which one you mean:

1) Boot USB image (OS + saved config):  nas backup
   Writes mountnas-backup-<host>-<date>.img.gz (default /mnt/nasdata/backups,
   or --to <dir|file>). COPY IT OFF THIS BOX. Restore = write it to another
   USB stick (Etcher/dd) and boot — never with two MountNAS sticks attached.
   Required before every nas upgrade.

2) Config snapshots (automatic):  nas rollback --list
   Every nas commit keeps the previous overlay on /cfg. Restore one with
   nas rollback <n> — applies at the next boot. This undoes a bad commit
   without touching the OS or your data.

3) Your DATA — neither of the above covers it. restic is baked in:
     restic init --repo sftp:user@host:/backups/nas
     restic backup --repo ... /mnt/disk1 /mnt/nasdata/appdata
   (works with local dirs, SFTP, S3, or any rclone remote; encrypted and
   deduplicated. Schedule it in cron and mail the summary — nas howto mail.)
